Rear Admiral Dr. Grace Murray Hopper

The Queen of Code

Grace Murray Hopper in her Navy uniform.

Important Events in Grace Murray Hopper's Life:

  • 1906: Born in New York City, U.S.
  • 1928: Graduated from Vassar College with a BA in mathematics and physics and joined the Vassar faculty.
  • 1930: Earned an MA in mathematics at Yale University. Also married to Vincent Foster Hopper, a Professor at New York University.
  • 1934: Earned a PhD in mathematics at Yale University; The same year her seminal dissertation New Types of Irreducibility Criteria was published.
  • 1941: Promoted to associate professor at Vassar.
  • 1943: Joined the United States Naval Reserve.
  • 1945: Became a widow.
  • 1949: Joined the Eckert-Mauchly Computer Corporation and provided businesses with computers.
  • 1952: Completed the “B-O” or “Flow-Matic” compiler. The Flow-Matic is considered the first compiler for the English language.
  • 1969: Awarded the first ever Computer Science Man-of-the-Year Award from the Data Processing Management Association.
  • 1971: The Sperry Corporation initiated an annual award in her name to honor young computer professionals for their significant contributions to computer science.
  • 1973: Became the first person from the United States and the first woman of any nationality to be made a Distinguished Fellow of the British Computer Society.
  • 1986: Officially retired from the U.S. Navy with the rank of Rear Admiral.
  • 1992: Passed away in her sleep in Arlington, Virginia.
  • 2016: Posthumously awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Barack Obama of the United States.
